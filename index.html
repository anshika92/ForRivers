Forgotten Rivers

They still flow. Scroll to learn the story they quietly tell

Once, rivers shaped where cities grew. We built over them, and slowly stopped seeing them. They still carry our rain, our runoff, and sadly much more. Today, this flow is no longer something we notice, but if we look closely it is still right in front of us

The blue highlight on the map shows the Bengaluru Urban District Boundary which covers an area larger than the BBMP

Bengaluru sits on a ridge that divides the city’s rainwater flow in two. This ridge is marked by the white line in the centre. Rain on your rooftop doesn’t stay put -

So where exactly does your rain go?

If your home lies on the left of the ridge line, it flows into the Arkavathi River system eventually joining Cauvery.

And if you live to the right of the ridge line, your rainwater flows toward the Dakshina Pinakini River, eventually making its way to the Bay of Bengal.

Have you ever stood by a river that carries rain from your neighborhood? Most of us haven't, because the rivers that once flowed around us have faded from view, hidden behind concrete, sewage, and new names. Just outside the city limits, to the south west, you will see a the blue dot on the map

At this spot, this is how the Vrishabhavathi River looks. Here it still resembles a river. But it bears the burden of a city that has forgotten it ever was one. Reduced to 'Kengeri Mori', it is treated as nothing more than a drain.
