Forgotten Rivers

They still flow.

Once, rivers shaped where cities grew.

We built over them, and stopped seeing them.

They still carry our rain, our runoff, and sadly much more.

Where does rain go?

The blue boundary on the map shows the Bangalore Urban District Boundary which is larger than the area that falls under BBMP

Bengaluru sits on a ridge that divides the city’s rainwater flow in two. This ridge is marked by the white line in the centre. Rain on your rooftop doesn’t stay put -

If your home lies on the left of the ridge line, it flows into the Arkavathi River system eventually joining Cauvery.

And if you live to the right of the ridge line, your rainwater flows toward the Dakshina Pinakini River, eventually making its way to the Bay of Bengal.
